# Vision, Direction & Passion Playbook

## The North Star
**Personalized Intelligence served on any body — human or robot.**

Build ML/AI systems that deliver personalized intelligence accessible on any device, edge station, or embodied agent. Become a principal-level engineer with the technical depth and business acumen to found or lead an enterprise in this space.

---

## Part 1: Execution Engine — Daily Skill Drills

Your drills are the reps that compound into career capital. Each drill targets a specific muscle needed at Staff+ level.

### Drill 1: Articulation
- **What:** Write or speak a 2-min explanation of a technical concept daily
- **Why:** Staff+ engineers sell ideas. If you can't articulate, you can't influence.
- **Resource:** Write a 1-pager or record a Loom each morning
- **Level up:** Present at team meetings, then org-wide, then conferences

### Drill 2: System Design
- **What:** Design one system per week (30-60 min whiteboard session)
- **Why:** Principal engineers own architecture decisions
- **Resource:** "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" (Kleppmann), System Design Interview books
- **Level up:** Review real production designs, write design docs for your team

### Drill 3: Early Phase Proposals
- **What:** Write one 1-page proposal per week for a new feature or system improvement
- **Why:** Staff+ engineers drive the roadmap, not just execute it
- **Resource:** Amazon 6-pager format, RFC templates
- **Level up:** Get proposals approved and funded

### Drill 4: Org Navigation & Influence
- **What:** Map stakeholders weekly, practice cross-team alignment
- **Why:** Principal engineers operate across org boundaries
- **Resource:** "Staff Engineer" (Will Larsen), "An Elegant Puzzle"
- **Level up:** Lead cross-team initiatives, build coalition for your proposals

### Drill 5: Business Impact & Value Proposition
- **What:** Size the impact of one opportunity per week (TAM, revenue impact, cost savings)
- **Why:** Enterprise builders think in business outcomes, not just code
- **Resource:** Industry reports (Gartner, a16z), company 10-Ks, competitive analysis
- **Level up:** Tie every project to a dollar amount or strategic metric

### Drill 6: Launch, Publicity & Impact Posts
- **What:** Write one impact summary or launch post per project milestone
- **Why:** Visibility = career velocity. Your work doesn't count if nobody knows.
- **Resource:** Internal launch templates, LinkedIn/blog posts, tech talks
- **Level up:** Build a public portfolio of shipped work

### Drill 7: Landing Change & Reliability
- **What:** Review one production incident or reliability metric weekly
- **Why:** Principal engineers own reliability. Shipping is nothing without uptime.
- **Resource:** SRE Book (Google), incident postmortems, chaos engineering
- **Level up:** Own SLOs for your systems, drive reliability improvements

### Drill 11: Production Hotfix
- **What:** Practice diagnosing and fixing a production issue weekly (real or simulated)
- **Why:** Staff+ engineers are the last line of defense. Speed and accuracy under pressure separate senior from principal.
- **How:** Pick a past incident (yours or public postmortem), time-box 30 min to: reproduce, root-cause, write the fix, validate it won't regress. Practice the full cycle: triage → diagnose → patch → verify → communicate.
- **Resource:** Public postmortems (Google, Meta, Cloudflare), SRE Book Ch14, your team's incident archive
- **Level up:** Lead incident response, reduce MTTR, build runbooks that prevent repeat incidents

### Drill 12: Production Metrics & SLA Quantification
- **What:** Pick one system weekly — define or audit its SLIs, SLOs, error budgets, and key behavior invariants
- **Why:** You can't own reliability if you can't measure it. Principal engineers quantify system health, not just feel it.
- **How:** For a service you own or interact with: identify the 3-5 SLIs that matter (latency P50/P99, error rate, throughput, saturation). Set SLO targets. Calculate error budget burn rate. Validate that dashboards/alerts actually match these definitions.
- **Resource:** Google SRE Workbook (Ch2-4), Datadog/Grafana SLO guides, "Implementing SLOs" (Alex Hidalgo)
- **Level up:** Own SLO reviews for your org, drive error-budget-based release decisions, build automated SLO reporting

### Drill 8: Personalized Intelligence Deep Dive
- **What:** Read one paper or implement one prototype per week in your passion area
- **Why:** This is your edge — depth in personalized AI + edge serving is rare
- **Resource:** arXiv (rec systems, edge ML, model compression), HuggingFace, MLX
- **Level up:** Publish findings, open-source prototypes, build demo systems

### Drill 9: Pre-IPO & Market Signal Analysis
- **What:** Review one emerging company per week — their market fit, funding, tech stack
- **Why:** Pattern recognition for enterprise opportunities
- **Resource:** Crunchbase, PitchBook, TechCrunch, a16z newsletters, Y Combinator
- **Level up:** Build thesis on where the market is going, identify gaps

### Drill 10: Lifestyle & Signal Integration
- **What:** Weekly reflection — connect dots between drills, market signals, and your trajectory
- **Why:** Without reflection, drills are just busywork
- **Resource:** Sunday 30-min journaling session
- **Level up:** Quarterly career reviews with mentor

## Part 2: Impact & Enterprise — The Builder's Path

### Impact Proposal Framework
For every project or initiative, answer:
1. **Problem:** What specific pain exists? Who feels it?
2. **Scale:** How many people/systems affected? What's the cost of inaction?
3. **Solution:** What do you propose? Why this approach?
4. **Differentiation:** Why is this better than alternatives?
5. **Metrics:** How will you measure success?
6. **Timeline:** What can you ship in 2 weeks? 2 months? 6 months?

### Enterprise Watchlist Categories
- **Edge AI / On-device ML** — Apple, Qualcomm, MediaTek, startups
- **Personalized AI** — recommendation engines, personal assistants, agents
- **Embodied AI / Robotics** — humanoid robots, manipulation, locomotion
- **Model Serving Infrastructure** — inference optimization, model compression
- **AI Platform Companies** — the picks-and-shovels plays

### Market Fit Signals to Watch
- Companies raising Series B+ in your domain
- Enterprise adoption patterns (who's buying what)
- Open-source momentum (GitHub stars, community growth)
- Talent movement (where are top researchers going?)
- Regulatory changes affecting AI deployment

## Part 3: Career Trajectory — Staff to Principal

### The Ladder
| Level | Focus | Income Range | Key Differentiator |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Senior | Owns features | $200-350K | Technical excellence |
| Staff | Owns systems | $350-500K | Cross-team influence |
| Senior Staff | Owns domains | $500-700K | Org-wide strategy |
| Principal | Owns direction | $700K-1.2M+ | Company-wide impact |

### What Gets You There
- **Technical depth** in a high-value domain (yours: personalized intelligence + edge AI)
- **Business impact** that leadership can point to
- **Influence radius** that extends beyond your team
- **Hiring magnet** — people want to work with you
- **Vision** that shapes the company's technical direction

## Gamification: The Quest Map

Think of your career as an RPG with skill trees:

### Skill Trees
- **Architect** (System Design, Reliability, Landing Change)
- **Strategist** (Proposals, Business Impact, Market Analysis)
- **Communicator** (Articulation, Launch Posts, Org Navigation)
- **Innovator** (Deep Dive, Prototyping, Pre-IPO Analysis)

Each drill completed = XP in its skill tree. Level up by consistency, not perfection.
